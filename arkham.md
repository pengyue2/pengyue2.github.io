## Design Focus

Stealth-driven level design with flexible transitions between stealth and combat.

---

## Core Mechanics

The Arkham series emphasizes predator-style stealth gameplay, supported by highly vertical level design.
Environments include vantage points, ground-level cover, tunnels, and multi-layered spaces,
each paired with distinct stealth actions and takedowns.

<img src="images/bak_map.jpeg" width="600">

*Vantage points, ceiling access, sewer gates, and destructible items are all highlighted in Detective Mode*  
([Image source](https://www.batman-arkham.com))

This spatial variety prevents stealth encounters from becoming single-solution puzzles
and instead rewards situational awareness and environmental reading.

Detective Mode, combined with a wide variety of gadgets, lowers the execution barrier
while encouraging players to experiment with different stealth approaches.

<img src="images/bak_tools.jpeg" width="600">

*Bat gadget selection interface in-game*
([Image source](https://www.batman-arkham.com))

By reducing information uncertainty, the game shifts difficulty away from memorization
and toward tactical decision-making.

## Design Strengths

The level design supports stealth across multiple spatial layers,
encouraging players to manipulate enemy positioning rather than rely on fixed routes.

The grappling gun and gliding mechanics enable rapid repositioning,
giving players recovery options that favor movement and planning over direct confrontation.

Importantly, stealth failure does not immediately force combat.
Instead, the game provides transitional mechanics that preserve player agency.

When detected, players are prompted to make quick decisions â€” such as using slow-motion aiming,
deploying smoke grenades, engaging briefly, or grappling away â€” allowing recovery rather than immediate failure.

These mechanics allow players to make mistakes without collapsing the stealth experience,
maintaining tension while reducing frustration.

High-quality free-flow combat ensures that encounters remain enjoyable even after stealth failure,
preventing detection from feeling like a hard penalty.

Enemy AI dynamically adapts to player tactics, gradually countering frequently used gadgets or approaches.
Enemies also react strongly to player presence, reinforcing fear as a readable and emotionally satisfying feedback system.

This combination discourages repetitive strategies while reinforcing the fantasy of controlling the battlefield.

## Design Insights

The Arkham series demonstrates how forgiving stealth systems can encourage experimentation.

By allowing recovery after mistakes and enabling smooth stealthâ€“combat transitions,
the game maintains tension without punishing failure,
making it particularly accessible to non-hardcore stealth players.

Adaptive enemy behavior further reinforces long-term engagement and tactical variety,
encouraging bold, expressive, and visually striking stealth playstyles
often shared by players through gameplay recordings.
